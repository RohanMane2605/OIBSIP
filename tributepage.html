Bharat Ratna Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar,born on 24 April (1973) is an Indian former international cricketer who captained the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket.Hailed as the world's most prolific batsman of all time, he is the all-time highest run-scorer in both ODI and Test cricket with more than 18,000 runs and 15,000 runs, respectively.He also holds the record for receiving the most player of the match awards in international cricket.Tendulkar was a Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha by nomination from 2012 to 2018.

Born : 24 April 1973 (age 50) Bombay, Maharashtra, India (now Mumbai, India)
Honours :
  • (1994)-Arjuna Award, by the Government of India in recognition of his outstanding achievement in sports.
  • (1997-98)-Khel Ratna Award, India's highest honour given for achievement in sports.
  • (1999)-Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award
  • (2001)-Maharashtra Bhushan Award, Maharashtra state's highest civilian award
  • (2008)-Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.
  • (2014)-Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award
    • Early Life : Sachin Tendulkar was born in Bombay on April 24, 1973, to a Maharashtrian family. His father, Ramesh Tendulkar, was a novelist, and his mother, Rajni, worked in insurance. He has three older siblings. Tendulkar grew up in Bandra (East) and was initially interested in tennis, idolizing John McEnroe. His brother Ajit introduced him to cricket in 1984, and he trained under coach Ramakant Achrekar. Tendulkar attended Sharadashram Vidyamandir School, where he made his cricket debut. He played club cricket and attended the MRF Pace Foundation in 1987 but was advised to focus on batting. He had notable early achievements in cricket, including a record partnership with Vinod Kambli in 1988.
    Cricket Career : Sachin Tendulkar had an illustrious cricket career spanning over two decades. He made his international debut for India at the age of 16 in 1989. Tendulkar is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket, holding numerous records including most runs scored in both Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket. He scored 100 international centuries, a feat unmatched by any other player. Tendulkar played a key role in India's World Cup victory in 2011 and retired from international cricket in 2013. Throughout his career, he received numerous awards and accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award,in 2014.
    Other Career :
    Business Direction : Apart from his cricketing career, Sachin Tendulkar has been involved in various other ventures. He has been an entrepreneur, involved in businesses ranging from sports management to hospitality. Tendulkar has also been active in philanthropy, supporting initiatives related to healthcare, education, and underprivileged children through his foundation. Additionally, he has been a brand ambassador for various companies and has made appearances in advertising campaigns. Tendulkar's influence extends beyond cricket, making him a prominent figure in Indian society and culture.
    Role in public awareness : Sachin Tendulkar has played a significant role in public awareness campaigns, particularly in promoting health and education initiatives. He has been associated with campaigns focusing on issues such as cancer awareness, road safety, and promoting sports among youth. Tendulkar's influence and popularity have been leveraged to raise awareness and encourage positive behavior change among the public, making him a prominent figure in social causes beyond the realm of sports.
